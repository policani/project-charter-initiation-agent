# Objectives, Deliverables, and Success Measures

Keep these categories separate.

## Business outcome

The business condition the project supports. Example: reduce customer onboarding cycle time.

## Project objective

The project-level result the team is expected to achieve. Example: deploy a standardized onboarding control workflow across three regions.

## Deliverable

The authorized output. Example: intake form, dashboard, standard operating procedure, training package, integration release.

## Success measure

The measure used to judge whether the work achieved the intended result. Example: reduce average onboarding cycle time from 18 days to 10 days by Q4.

## Acceptance condition

The evidence required to close the project. Example: sponsor signs off that dashboard, process, training, and handoff are complete.
