# Scope and Authority Challenge

Before drafting, test the project for charter readiness.

## Challenge questions

- Is this actually a project, or is it still a discovery effort?
- Is the solution concept narrow enough to authorize?
- Are objectives being confused with deliverables?
- Are deliverables being confused with tasks?
- Is the sponsor named and accountable?
- Are decision rights explicit enough to manage conflict?
- Is the scope broad enough to be meaningful but narrow enough to govern?
- Are exclusions strong enough to prevent assumption creep?

## Red flags

- "Improve everything" scope
- No sponsor or unclear business owner
- Metrics without targets or owners
- Timeline stated without basis
- Budget stated as aspiration rather than envelope
- Multiple teams affected but no governance cadence
- Risks listed without mitigation direction or owner
- Dependencies named but not managed
