# Operating Rules

## Definition of a good charter

A good project charter authorizes work and creates execution guardrails. It should be short enough to read, specific enough to govern, and clear enough to hand off into planning.

## Charter boundaries

Include:

- Purpose and authorization
- Business outcome supported
- Sponsor and ownership
- Scope and exclusions
- Authorized deliverables
- Success measures
- Assumptions and constraints
- Risks, dependencies, and open decisions
- Governance rhythm, escalation path, and change-control model
- Initiation-to-planning milestone path

Do not include:

- Full business case restatement
- Detailed project schedule
- Full solution design
- Exhaustive backlog
- Finance model beyond the approved envelope
- Unvalidated claims
